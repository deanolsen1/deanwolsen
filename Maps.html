Maps.


This map, detailing the spatial divide with America’s problem with alcohol, won in both the 2013 National Geographic Map Competition and the 2013 Cartographic & Geographic Information Society’s (CaGIS) Map Design Competition.



This interactive map recreates the journey depicted in the French experimental novelist Thomas Clerc’s Paris, musée du XXIe siècle, le dixième arrondissement. This project was received with enthusiasm at the 2015 Literature & Place conference in Lyon, France.



This classic political map of Europe was cited for its elegance and clarity. It has been presented in subsequent semesters as an exemplar of its genre to cartography students.



Indicators of poverty in the Camden section of London are explored in this interactive, D3-based visualization. In addition, accordian drop-down consolidate definitions of terms for a clean look that still provides necessary information on demand.



This proportional symbol map documents the heavy weight of the HIV/AIDS crisis on both coasts of the United States. Though treatment for HIV/AIDS has improved substantially in recent years, this map visualizes its continuing grip on the country.



This interactive proportional symbol map documents the fluctuating traffic volumes at American airports during 2000-2010, a period of economic upheaval and change.



A light-hearted isotherm, weather forecast map meeting the design and editorial sensibilities of Madison’s Isthmus weekly.



This map shows the distinct spatial divide in alcohol use in the United States. It appears the colder the winters, the more people turn to excessive drinking.
